How to Make
Fresh Lemonade

Nothing beats a tall, ice-cold glass of homemade lemonade on a warm day. Simple ingredients, five minutes, and pure refreshment — every single time.

The Method

Step-by-Step Instructions

Follow these simple steps for a perfectly balanced, refreshingly tart lemonade.

01

Wash and Cut the Lemons

Rinse your lemons under cool water, scrubbing gently. Roll them on the counter with your palm to loosen the juice inside, then slice each in half.

02

Squeeze the Lemon Juice

Using a citrus juicer or your hands, squeeze the juice from all lemons into a large bowl or pitcher. Aim for about ¾ cup of fresh juice.

03

Add Sugar and Mix Well

Add the sugar directly to the lemon juice while it's at its most acidic — this helps it dissolve faster. Stir vigorously until no granules remain.

04

Pour in Cold Water and Stir

Add 4 cups of cold water to your lemon-sugar mixture. Stir well to combine everything into a smooth, balanced lemonade base.

05

Add Ice Cubes and Mint Leaves

Fill glasses generously with ice cubes. If using mint, lightly press the leaves between your fingers first to release their aroma, then add to the glass.

06

Serve Chilled 🎉

Pour lemonade over the ice, garnish with a lemon slice on the rim, and enjoy immediately. Best served on a warm sunny day!

Pro Advice

Tips for the Best Lemonade

Small details make a big difference. Here's what the experts know.

🍋

Use Fresh Lemons

Bottled juice simply can't compete. Fresh lemons bring a brightness and complexity that makes every sip taste alive.

🍯

Adjust the Sweetness

Sugar is personal. Start with less, taste, and add more gradually. You can also swap for honey or a simple syrup for a smoother sweetness.

❄️

Chill Before Serving

For a party-perfect pitcher, make the lemonade base ahead and refrigerate for at least an hour. The flavors meld beautifully when cold.
